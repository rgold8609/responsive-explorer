So... You want to become an animator, huh?

That's a great idea, but not really an easy one. Because if you think art is hard then hoo boy is animation not even remotely in the same ball park. It's basically drawing but you need to understand physics. If that sounds like a nightmare beyond comprehension, don't worry because like physics, you can learn it just like anyone else. Unlike physics, you don't need to know math. Big win for non-masochists everywhere!


Why become animator?

Honestly, why not? It's a great skill to have. Fun, interesting, and uncommon, animation is a great thing to boast to your friends about. "Oh, you think you're cool? I just animate you getting mugged. How do you like that?" See? Just think of the possiblities. Plus, animation is in depressingly short supply. All these companies think AI will take over animation, yeah? Just look at the reception of anything animated with AI. We ain't getting replaced any time soon.


Autonomy

Even if AI won't replace animation for a while, still, crap happens. Companies sure love random mass-layoffs (I swear, it's like a biweekly occurance. "Oh, it's Tuesday again. Steve, you're fired"). But who said anything about working for a souless coorperation? Not me.

Indie animation is a profitable option in high demand. If you haven't heard the world in general talk about one new big animated series a month, you live under a shipping container at the bottom of the Pacific Ocean. The best place to do animation is on YouTube. Even the least remarkable animations have thousands and thousands of views. With practice and a bit of luck, you can obtain a little thing I like to call... profitable job security (yay).


How do you learn to animate?

Learning animation is, as previously established, not easy. Luckily, it's just skill. You aren't born an animator, you become one, and if you're looking to become one, you've come to the right place!
